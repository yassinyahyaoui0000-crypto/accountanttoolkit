How we evaluated these tools

We evaluated each tool from the specific perspective of a freelance accountant or bookkeeper managing their own practice — not a small business owner using accounting software for their shop. The criteria that mattered most were invoicing quality and flexibility, recurring billing for retainer clients, accounting depth underneath the invoicing layer, ease of bank reconciliation, and overall value for a solo practitioner.

We signed up for each tool's free trial, created test invoices, and evaluated the actual user experience rather than just reading the marketing pages.

X

Xero

Best overall for freelance accountants

🥇 Best overall

Xero is our top pick for established freelance accountants because it's the only tool on this list that combines genuinely excellent invoicing with professional-grade accounting underneath. You're not just getting a billing tool — you're getting a full accounting platform that happens to have great invoicing built in.

The invoicing itself is clean and fast. You can create branded invoices in under two minutes, set up automatic recurring invoices for retainer clients, and configure automatic payment reminders at customizable intervals. Clients can pay directly from the invoice via credit card, direct debit, or bank transfer.

What separates Xero from the other options is what happens after the invoice is paid — automatic bank reconciliation that matches payments to invoices intelligently, full double-entry accounting, and financial reports that actually mean something to someone with an accounting background.

Best for: Freelance accountants with an established client base who want proper accounting depth alongside their invoicing.

FreshBooks

Best pure invoicing experience

🥈 Best invoicing focus

FreshBooks has the most polished invoicing interface of any tool we tested. If your primary concern is sending professional invoices quickly and getting paid reliably, FreshBooks is hard to beat. The invoice creation flow is genuinely fast — you can send a professional, branded invoice in under 90 seconds once your client and service details are set up.

The time tracking integration is also excellent. If you bill hourly, you can track time directly in FreshBooks and convert time entries to invoice line items with one click. For bookkeepers who charge by the hour, this alone is worth the subscription.

FreshBooks Plus adds double-entry accounting, making it a credible accounting tool as well as an invoicing one. It's not as deep as Xero but covers everything most solo practitioners need for their own books.

Best for: Freelance accountants who prioritize a polished invoicing and client-facing experience, especially hourly billers.

B

Bonsai

Best all-in-one client workflow

🥉 Best for new freelancers

Bonsai stands out from everything else on this list because it's not just invoicing software — it's a complete client workflow platform. You can create a proposal, get it signed as a contract with e-signature, track time against the project, and generate an invoice, all without leaving the platform.

For a new freelance accountant who is still building their client onboarding process, Bonsai removes a lot of friction. The contract templates are professionally written and legally sound, which matters when you're taking on your first paying clients and don't want to spend money on a lawyer to draft a service agreement.

The accounting side is basic — single-entry, limited reporting — so if you need proper double-entry bookkeeping for your own practice finances, you'll want a separate tool for that. But as a client-facing invoicing and workflow platform, Bonsai is excellent value.

W

Wave

Best free invoicing option

💰 Best free option

Wave is genuinely free — not a free trial, not a freemium with key features locked. The core accounting, invoicing, and receipt scanning are free forever with no invoice or client limits. For a freelance accountant just starting out who wants zero overhead while building their first few clients, Wave is a legitimate choice.

The invoicing is better than you'd expect from a free tool. Professional templates, recurring invoices, automatic payment reminders, and online payment collection are all included. The catch is payment processing fees — if clients pay by card you pay 2.9% + $0.60 per transaction, which adds up at scale.

Wave's accounting is single-entry and the reporting is limited, but for tracking basic income and expenses it works. The bank connection via Plaid works reliably for most major banks.

Best for: Freelance accountants just starting out who want professional invoicing at zero cost while they get established.

Zoho Books

Best value for money

⚙️ Best value

Zoho Books is the most underrated tool on this list. At $15/month for the Standard plan, you get double-entry accounting, unlimited invoices, bank reconciliation, project tracking, and a client portal — features that cost significantly more on FreshBooks or Xero. If budget is a consideration, Zoho Books deserves serious attention.

The invoicing is clean and professional, with good template customization, recurring invoice support, and automatic reminders. The automation features are particularly strong — you can set up workflow rules to automatically send invoices, reminders, and receipts based on triggers, which saves real time for a busy solo practitioner.

The main downside is the learning curve. Zoho Books is feature-dense and can feel overwhelming initially compared to the cleaner interfaces of FreshBooks or Bonsai. But once you're familiar with it, the depth is genuinely impressive for the price.

How to choose the right one

Rather than recommending one tool for everyone, here's a decision framework based on where you are in your practice:

  • Just starting, 1–3 clients: Start with Wave (free) or Bonsai (if you need contracts). No point paying $33–42/month before you're billing consistently.
  • Established, 5–15 clients: Move to Xero or FreshBooks. The accounting depth and automation pay for themselves in saved time.
  • Budget-conscious but want accounting depth: Zoho Books is genuinely excellent and significantly cheaper than Xero or FreshBooks.
  • Planning to scale beyond solo: QuickBooks' brand recognition and integration ecosystem become more valuable as you grow.

One tip: Whatever you choose, sign up for the free trial during a quiet week and actually use it to invoice a real client. How a tool feels when you're under time pressure is a better indicator than any feature comparison table.
