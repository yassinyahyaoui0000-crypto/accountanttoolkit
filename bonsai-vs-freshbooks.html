What makes these two different

At first glance, Bonsai and FreshBooks look similar — both are cloud-based tools for independent professionals, both handle invoicing, and both sit in a similar price range. But they come from very different product philosophies.

Bonsai started as a contracts and proposals tool for freelancers and expanded into invoicing and bookkeeping. The result is a platform that covers the entire client lifecycle — from first proposal to final invoice — but with shallower accounting features than a dedicated accounting tool.

FreshBooks started as an invoicing tool and built accounting features on top. It now offers double-entry accounting, more detailed reporting, and deeper financial tracking — but the client management side is thinner than Bonsai's.

For a solo bookkeeper, the question is really: do you need the full client workflow (contracts, proposals, time tracking, invoicing) or do you need serious accounting depth?

Pricing: what you actually pay

Bonsai pricing

Bonsai's Starter plan at $21/month covers invoicing, contracts, proposals, and basic time tracking for up to 3 clients. The Professional plan at $32/month removes client limits and adds more advanced features. The Business plan at $66/month adds subcontractor management. For most solo bookkeepers the Professional plan is the right tier.

Bonsai regularly offers discounts for annual billing — typically 25% off, bringing the Professional plan closer to $24/month effective.

FreshBooks pricing

FreshBooks starts at $19/month for the Lite plan but caps you at 5 billable clients — too restrictive for most active bookkeepers. The Plus plan at $33/month allows 50 clients and adds double-entry accounting, which is where most practitioners should start. The Premium plan at $60/month is unlimited clients.

Like Bonsai, FreshBooks offers significant annual billing discounts and frequently runs promotional pricing for new signups.

Invoicing: both are excellent

This is the one area where both tools genuinely shine and the difference is minimal. Both offer professional invoice templates, automatic payment reminders, recurring invoices, and online payment collection via credit card and bank transfer.

FreshBooks has a slight edge on invoice customization — more template options and more granular control over the visual appearance. Bonsai's invoices are clean and professional but slightly less customizable.

Where Bonsai pulls ahead is the connection between invoices and the rest of the client workflow. You can create a proposal, have the client approve it, convert it to a contract, track time against the project, and generate an invoice — all without leaving the platform. FreshBooks handles the invoicing end well but requires third-party tools for proposals and contracts.

For solo bookkeepers specifically: If your clients pay on retainer and you just need to send the same invoice every month, both tools handle this equally well. If you're constantly onboarding new clients with custom scopes of work, Bonsai's proposal-to-invoice workflow saves significant time.

Accounting depth: FreshBooks wins clearly

This is the most important difference for someone with an accounting background.

FreshBooks Plus offers proper double-entry accounting — the foundation of accurate bookkeeping. You get a full chart of accounts, journal entries, a proper balance sheet, profit and loss statement, and a trial balance. For a freelance bookkeeper managing their own practice finances, this is the correct way to track your numbers.

Bonsai's accounting is single-entry and designed more for freelancers who want a simple income/expense overview than for accounting professionals who understand what double-entry means. The reports are simpler and less detailed. You won't find a proper balance sheet or trial balance in Bonsai.

If you're a bookkeeper, you know this matters. Using single-entry accounting to manage your own practice's finances is a bit like a chef eating fast food — technically fine, but professionally uncomfortable.

Client management: Bonsai wins clearly

Bonsai was built for the full client lifecycle and it shows. The platform includes a CRM-style client management system where you can track all client communication, projects, contracts, and invoices in one place. You can store client details, set up client portals, and manage multiple projects per client.

The contracts feature alone is worth highlighting — Bonsai includes legally-vetted contract templates for various freelance scenarios, with e-signature built in. For a solo bookkeeper taking on new clients, having a proper engagement letter or service agreement signed before starting work is essential. Bonsai makes this frictionless. FreshBooks has no equivalent.

FreshBooks does have a client section but it's more of an address book than a CRM. You can see all invoices for a client and send them statements, but there's no contract management, proposal tracking, or project pipeline view.
