Editorial policy

This page explains how AccountantToolkit approaches software reviews, pricing claims, updates, corrections, and commercial pressure. The site is small, but the editorial standard should still be clear.

Updated March 19, 2026 Trust page

Editorial principle

Useful guidance matters more than maximizing clicks, commissions, or inflated product scores.

How product pages are researched

AccountantToolkit pages are written around practical buyer questions: what the tool is actually good at, where it falls short, how pricing should be interpreted, and what kind of user should skip it entirely.

For pricing, trials, free plans, and plan names, the site prefers official vendor pages over third-party summaries. When vendor pricing is promotional, region-specific, or usage-based, the copy is softened rather than made falsely precise.

How pages are updated

Each article should carry a visible updated or reviewed date. Updates are meant to reflect real content review, not fake freshness.

Major updates should happen when a product's plan structure, positioning, trial flow, or recommended use case changes enough to affect the buying guidance.

How corrections work

If a factual claim is wrong, especially around pricing or product capability, the site should correct it promptly and update the page review date when the correction materially changes the article.

The contact page explains what kinds of correction requests belong here.

How commercial relationships are handled

Affiliate relationships, if present, should be disclosed clearly and should not determine rankings. The site's commercial disclosure lives on the affiliate disclosure page and should be linked from relevant review and comparison content.
