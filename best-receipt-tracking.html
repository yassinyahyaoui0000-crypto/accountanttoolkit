Why receipt tracking matters more for accountants

As a self-employed accountant, you face a slightly ironic challenge: you advise clients on meticulous record-keeping while managing your own business expenses in the background. The tools you use for your own practice need to be reliable, accurate, and integrate cleanly with whatever accounting software you're running.

Beyond the obvious tax deduction angle, good receipt tracking gives you clean expense data for your own profit and loss reporting — which matters if you're tracking practice profitability or planning to grow. A receipt tracking app that misreads amounts or fails to categorize correctly creates reconciliation work that costs you more time than it saves.

Important: Tax authorities in most countries require you to keep business expense records for 5–7 years. A receipt tracking app is not just convenient — for a self-employed professional it's a compliance requirement. Choose a tool you'll actually use consistently.

What we evaluated

We tested each app by scanning identical sets of receipts — including crumpled paper receipts, digital PDF receipts, restaurant bills, and supplier invoices — and measured OCR extraction accuracy for amount, date, merchant name, and tax amounts. We also evaluated mobile app quality, integration depth with major accounting platforms, and overall value for a solo practitioner.

D

Dext (formerly Receipt Bank)

Best overall receipt tracking for accountants

🥇 Best overall

Dext is the professional standard for receipt and document capture in the accounting world — it's what most accounting firms use for client document collection. For a self-employed accountant managing their own expenses, it brings that same professional-grade accuracy to your personal practice management.

The OCR accuracy is genuinely the best we tested. Dext correctly extracted the amount, date, merchant, and tax on 94% of receipts in our testing, compared to 78–85% for the free alternatives. On crumpled or low-quality receipts, the gap was even larger. For an accountant who needs clean data going into their accounting software, that accuracy difference translates directly into less manual correction time.

The integration with Xero, QuickBooks, and Sage is deep and reliable — extracted data flows through automatically with correct categorization. You can also set up rules so that receipts from specific suppliers are always assigned to the right expense category without manual intervention.

X

Xero Expenses

Best built-in option for Xero users

🔗 Best built-in

If you're already running Xero for your practice accounting, Xero Expenses is the most frictionless receipt tracking option available. There's no separate app to manage, no integration to configure, and no additional login. You photograph a receipt in the Xero mobile app and it flows directly into your Xero account with the data pre-extracted.

The OCR accuracy is good — not quite at Dext's level but significantly better than Wave's free offering. For most receipts you'll get clean extraction of the amount, date, and merchant. The categorization suggestions improve over time as Xero learns your patterns.

The main limitation is that Xero Expenses is an add-on on some plans — check whether it's included in your current Xero subscription before assuming it's free. On the Standard plan it's typically included for one user, which covers most solo practitioners.

W

Wave Receipts

Best free receipt scanning

💰 Best free

Wave's receipt scanning is part of the free Wave accounting platform and is surprisingly capable for a zero-cost tool. You photograph a receipt with the Wave mobile app, and the OCR extracts the key data and creates an expense transaction in your Wave account automatically.

The accuracy is adequate for most standard receipts — printed supermarket receipts, restaurant bills, and supplier invoices all extract reliably. Where it struggles is with handwritten receipts, crumpled paper, or unusual receipt formats. For a self-employed accountant with mostly digital or standard printed receipts, it handles the job.

The main limitation is that Wave Receipts only works within the Wave ecosystem. If you're using Xero, QuickBooks, or another accounting platform, Wave Receipts is useless to you — there's no export or integration.

Z

Zoho Expense

Best value for money

⚙️ Best value

Zoho Expense is a dedicated expense management tool — separate from Zoho Books but integrating seamlessly with it — that offers impressive functionality at a very competitive price. The free plan covers receipt scanning for up to 5 users with 5GB storage, which is enough for most solo practitioners just starting out.

The OCR accuracy is solid and the automation features are particularly strong for the price. You can set up expense policies, automatic approval workflows, and recurring expense rules that reduce manual processing time significantly. The mileage tracking using GPS is one of the better implementations we tested.

Zoho Expense integrates with Zoho Books, Xero, and QuickBooks, giving it more flexibility than Wave Receipts. If you're using Zoho Books as your accounting platform, the integration is native and seamless.

H

Hubdoc

Best for automatic document fetching

📄 Best for documents

Hubdoc solves a different problem from the other tools on this list. Rather than just scanning receipts you photograph, Hubdoc automatically fetches financial documents — bank statements, utility bills, supplier invoices — from your online accounts and organizes them in one place. For a self-employed accountant who gets most of their expense documentation digitally, this is extremely useful.

You connect Hubdoc to your bank, your regular suppliers, and any other accounts that generate financial documents. Hubdoc logs in automatically and pulls down new documents as they appear. Combined with manual receipt uploads, it creates a comprehensive document capture system with minimal ongoing effort.

Hubdoc is included free with Xero subscriptions, which makes it effectively zero additional cost for Xero users. The Xero integration is the strongest — documents flow directly into Xero with extracted data pre-filled.

Which one should you use?

The right choice depends almost entirely on what accounting software you're running and how many receipts you process per month:

  • Using Xero: Start with Hubdoc (free with your subscription) for document fetching, and Xero Expenses for receipt scanning. If you need higher OCR accuracy, add Dext.
  • Using Wave: Use Wave Receipts — it's free and integrates natively. No reason to pay for something separate.
  • Using Zoho Books: Use Zoho Expense — the native integration is seamless and the free plan covers most solo practitioners.
  • High receipt volume, need maximum accuracy: Dext is worth the $20/month. The time saved on manual corrections pays for itself quickly.
  • Just starting out, minimal expenses: Wave Receipts or Zoho Expense free plan. Upgrade to Dext when your practice grows.

The most common mistake: Choosing a receipt app that doesn't integrate with your accounting software. You end up double-entering data manually, which defeats the entire purpose. Always start with your accounting platform and work backwards to the receipt tool.
