FreshBooks Review for Freelancers (2025): Honest Assessment

FreshBooks has been around since 2003 and remains one of the most popular invoicing and accounting tools for freelancers. But popularity doesn't mean it's the right fit for everyone — especially freelance accountants who understand the difference between a proper accounting platform and a polished invoicing tool. Here's our honest take after hands-on testing.

What FreshBooks actually is

FreshBooks markets itself as accounting software, but it's more accurate to call it a premium invoicing platform with accounting features bolted on. That's not a criticism — it's what makes it excellent at what it does. The invoicing experience is the best we've tested across any tool in this price range. The accounting depth, while adequate on the Plus plan and above, doesn't match dedicated accounting platforms like Xero or QuickBooks.

For a freelance accountant, this distinction matters. If your priority is a polished, professional-looking client-facing experience — beautiful invoices, easy payment collection, smooth time tracking — FreshBooks excels. If your priority is deep accounting functionality for your own practice's books, you may eventually outgrow it.

The Lite plan's 5-client limit is genuinely too restrictive for any active freelance accountant — don't start there. The Plus plan at $33/month is where you want to be, and it's the plan most freelancers actually need. FreshBooks frequently runs 60–70% off promotions for the first 4–6 months, so check their current pricing before signing up.

Annual billing saves ~10%. If you're committing to FreshBooks, paying annually brings the Plus plan to around $29/month effective. FreshBooks also offers a 30-day free trial — use the full 30 days before deciding.

Invoicing: the best in class

FreshBooks' invoicing is genuinely exceptional. Creating an invoice takes under 90 seconds once your client and service details are set up. The templates are professional and fully customizable — you can add your logo, change colors, and adjust the layout to match your brand. The result looks like something a design agency would produce, not something generated by accounting software.

What works particularly well

Recurring invoices are frictionless to set up — specify the amount, frequency, and start date, and FreshBooks handles everything from there. For retainer clients, this is genuinely set-and-forget.

Automatic payment reminders can be configured to send at specific intervals before and after the due date. You control the message and timing. For freelance accountants who hate chasing invoices, this alone saves meaningful time every month.

Online payment collection is built in — clients can pay directly from the invoice via credit card or bank transfer. The payment processing fees apply (2.9% + $0.30 for cards, 1% for ACH bank transfers), but the convenience of having payment embedded in the invoice noticeably improves collection rates.

Time tracking: best integration we've tested

If you bill hourly, FreshBooks' time tracking integration is the strongest of any tool we reviewed. You can track time directly in FreshBooks — either with a running timer or manual entry — assign time to specific clients and projects, and then convert time entries to invoice line items with one click. No export, no copy-pasting, no manual calculation.

For a freelance bookkeeper who charges by the hour, this workflow alone can justify the subscription cost in time saved per month.

Accounting depth: adequate but not exceptional

FreshBooks Plus adds double-entry accounting, which is a meaningful upgrade from the Lite plan. You get a proper chart of accounts, journal entries, balance sheet, P&L statement, and trial balance. Bank feeds connect via Plaid and import transactions automatically.

The bank reconciliation works but is less intelligent than Xero's. FreshBooks suggests matches but doesn't learn your categorization patterns as quickly. Expect more manual intervention than you'd need in Xero, especially in the first month.

The reporting suite covers the essentials — P&L, balance sheet, accounts aging, expense reports, tax summaries. It's adequate for managing your own practice's finances, but less customizable than QuickBooks or Xero if you need sophisticated financial analysis.

What FreshBooks is missing

For a freelance accountant specifically, a few gaps are worth noting:

  • No contracts or proposals — you'll need a separate tool like Bonsai or HelloSign for client agreements
  • No inventory tracking — not relevant for service businesses but worth noting
  • The Lite plan's 5-client limit is surprisingly restrictive given the price
  • Multi-currency requires the Premium plan — if you have international clients, factor this into your cost calculation
  • Limited customization on reports compared to QuickBooks or Xero
