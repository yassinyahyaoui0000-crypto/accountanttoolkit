Next step

Keep the cleanup moving.

CSV export is free for now while the tool is in beta. If you want to turn the cleaned data into bookkeeping work, these are the most practical next tools to evaluate.

CSV columns

  • Dates: raw date, normalized date, and optional posted date.
  • Money: amount, debit, credit, and balance when available.
  • Audit trail: transaction type, row order, and original source line.

Known limits

  • Scanned-image PDFs still need OCR before parsing.
  • Ambiguous dates may need a different day-first or month-first setting.
  • Very custom bank layouts can still need manual cleanup after export.

Why this shape fits the repo

This site is a static publishing surface. A browser-based tool page keeps deployment simple on Cloudflare Pages and avoids introducing backend risk into the editorial pages.

How it works

Small architecture now, room to grow later.

The current build is intentionally local-first. If the tool later needs OCR, saved jobs, or institution-specific rules, that is the point to graduate into Cloudflare Pages Functions or a Worker-backed pipeline.

Step 1

Extract text

The tool reads a text-based PDF in the browser with PDF.js, then rebuilds rough statement lines from positioned text items.

Step 2

Infer transactions

Rows are identified from leading date patterns and amount tokens, with balance-aware heuristics used to improve debit and credit inference.

Step 3

Download CSV

The parser generates a flat CSV that can feed spreadsheet cleanup, bookkeeping imports, or a manual reconciliation pass.
